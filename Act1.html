The Story in the Book

Chapter 1

            Molly had been staring out of her window for about an hour now. On her desk, lying between the copies of Nature, New Scientist journals her work had appeared in, was a well-thumbed copy of On the Road. It had been Molly's favorite book since college, and the longer she spent in these four walls, the more she felt she needed to be free.

            That work is the refuge she had spent the last ten years in this room, sitting under a poster with an Oscar Wilde quote proclaiming of people who have nothing better to do. Although many considered her pioneering work unraveling the secret of the llama DNA to be an outstanding achievement, Molly did think she had something better to do.
