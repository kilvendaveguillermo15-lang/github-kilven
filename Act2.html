Pakbet (Filipino Vegetable Stew)

Pakbet is a classic Filipino dish featuring a medley of vegetables simmered in a savory broth flavored with bagoong (fermented fish sauce). It's hearty, nutritious, and perfect for a family meal. This recipe serves 4-6 people and takes about 45 minutes to prepare.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly or pork shoulder, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1/4 cup bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste), or to taste
  • 2 cups water or broth
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced and seeded
  • 10 okra, trimmed
  • 1/2 lb string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 small pumpkin (kalabasa), cubed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Fried tofu or shrimp for added protein

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© the garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the pork slices and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the tomatoes and bagoong. Cook for another 2 minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Pour in the water or broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Add the harder vegetables first: pumpkin and eggplant. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the bitter melon, okra, and string beans. Continue simmering for another 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed (bagoong is salty, so add sparingly).
  8. If using, add fried tofu or shrimp in the last 2 minutes to heat through.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy!

Tip: For a vegetarian version, omit the pork and use vegetable broth. Adjust bagoong for desired saltiness. Pakbet tastes even better the next day!
